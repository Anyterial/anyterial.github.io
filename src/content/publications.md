# Publications

This page lists key publications related to the databases and tools made available through Anyterial.

## Databases

### The ADAQ database

These publications contributed to the database and key methodology.

- [ADAQ: Automatic workflows for magneto-optical properties of point defects in semiconductors](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cpc.2021.108091), J. Davidsson, V. Ivády, R. Armiento, and I. A. Abrikosov, Computer Physics Communications, 108091 (2021).

Also see the [ADAQ publications page](https://defects.anyterial.se/publications).

### The Altermagnets database

- [High-Throughput Quantification of Altermagnetic Band Splitting](https://journals.aps.org/prmaterials/abstract/10.1103/mmdm-hrj4), Ali Sufyan, Brahim Marfoua, J. Andreas Larsson, E. van Loon, and R. Armiento, Phys. Rev. Mater. 10, 044407 (2026).

Also see the [Altermagnets database how-to-cite page](https://altermagnets.anyterial.se/how-to-cite).

### The Hard-coating Alloys DataBase (HADB)

- HADB: A materials-property database for hard-coating alloys, H. Levämäki, F. Bock, D. G. Sangiovanni, L. J. S. Johnson, F. Tasnádi, R. Armiento, I. A. Abrikosov, Thin Solid Films 766, 139627 (2023).

Also see the [HADB front page](https://hadb.funmat-ii.se/).

### Anyterial Crystals

We are about to launch a crystal structure database based on the following publication:

- [Screening 39 billion protostructures for materials discovery](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.21393), A. S Parackal, F. Trybel, F. A. Faber, R. Armiento, arXiv:2601.21393 [cond-mat.mtrl-sci] (2026).

### ADAQ software platform

Automatic Defect Analysis and Qualification (ADAQ) is a software platform for automatic workflows for high-throughput calculations of point defects in semiconductors.

**Main publication**

- [ADAQ: Automatic workflows for magneto-optical properties of point defects in semiconductors](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cpc.2021.108091), J. Davidsson, V. Ivády, R. Armiento, and I. A. Abrikosov, Computer Physics Communications, 108091 (2021).

**Tools**

- [ADAQ-SYM: Automated Symmetry Analysis of Defect Orbitals](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.04381), W. Stenlund, J. Davidsson, V. Ivády, R. Armiento, and I. A. Abrikosov, arXiv:2307.04381 [cond-mat.mtrl-sci] (2023).
